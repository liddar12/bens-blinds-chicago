Custom window treatments are cut to your exact specifications — which means measurement errors are permanent. Unlike paint or flooring that can be touched up, a custom shade cut 1/2 inch too narrow cannot be fixed. It gets replaced at your cost. Professional measurement is free with any Ben's Blinds installation, and we strongly recommend it for any Chicago home. But for homeowners who want to understand the process — or who are considering DIY installation — here's the professional approach.

## Inside Mount vs. Outside Mount: The First Decision

Before measuring, you must decide whether you're doing an inside mount (treatment fits within the window frame) or outside mount (treatment mounts on the wall or trim outside the frame). This decision affects everything about how you measure.

- Inside mount: Cleaner look, shade fits within frame. Requires adequate inside-mount depth (typically 2.5"+ for most shades, 3.5"+ for blinds). Must be done precisely — no forgiveness on width.
- Outside mount: More forgiving, covers frame edges, works when inside depth is insufficient. Width and height can be specified larger than the opening. Common in Chicago vintage buildings where frame depth is inadequate.

## Why Chicago Windows Are Tricky to Measure

Chicago's housing stock — dominated by buildings constructed between 1880 and 1970 — creates measurement challenges that newer construction doesn't have. Out-of-square frames are extremely common: measure width at top, middle, and bottom and you'll often find a 1/4–1/2 inch variation. Varying inside-mount depths are common, particularly in bay windows where the center pane often has more depth than the angled side panes. Original frame materials may be deteriorating in ways that affect whether inside-mount is even advisable.

## Measuring for Inside Mount

For inside-mount window treatments, measure the following:

- Width: Measure at three heights — top, middle, and bottom of the frame. Record the narrowest measurement. This is your width specification. Deductions for clearance are made by the manufacturer.
- Height: Measure at three widths — left, center, and right. Record the shortest measurement. This is your height specification.
- Depth: Measure the inside-mount depth — the distance from the front face of the frame to the window glass or stop. Compare to the minimum depth required by your chosen product (typically listed in the product specs).
- Squareness: If width varies more than 1/4" from top to bottom, note this. It may affect whether inside mount is advisable.

## Measuring for Outside Mount

For outside-mount treatments, you have more flexibility but need to make deliberate choices:

- Width: Decide how far beyond the frame edge you want the treatment to extend on each side (typically 1.5"–3" per side for light control and aesthetics). Add this to the frame width to get your mount width. Specify mount width, not frame width.
- Height: Decide where the mount will go (on trim, above trim, or above window) and how far down you want the treatment to extend. Measure from mount point to desired bottom of treatment.
- Note: Outside mount width is the width you're ordering, not a rough measurement requiring deduction.

## Bay Windows: The Chicago Special Case

Chicago greystone bay windows require separate measurement of each pane (typically three) because each pane has different dimensions and mounting constraints. The center pane is usually larger; the side panes are typically angled at 30–45 degrees from the wall. Each pane needs its own width, height, and depth measurement. The side panes often have shallower inside-mount depth than the center. For bay windows, we universally recommend in-person professional measurement rather than homeowner measurement — the combination of angled panes, varying depths, and the need for coordinated aesthetics across all three openings makes this a situation where measurement error is particularly likely.
