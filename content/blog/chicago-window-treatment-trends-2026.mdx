We install window treatments in hundreds of Chicago homes each year across every neighborhood — from River North high-rises to Oak Park craftsman bungalows to Pilsen row houses. That gives us a real-time view of what Chicago homeowners are actually choosing, not what design magazines suggest they should want. Here's what's genuinely trending in Chicago window treatments in 2026.

## 1. Motorized Everything (Especially in the West Loop)

Motorization has crossed from premium luxury to mainstream expectation in Chicago's loft market. The West Loop, Fulton Market, and River North neighborhoods — with their oversized industrial windows and tech-savvy resident demographics — have been early adopters, but the trend is spreading to Lincoln Park, Lakeview, and Gold Coast as well. Somfy battery motors have made motorization practical without hardwiring, and Alexa integration means voice control setup takes minutes. We're seeing motorization specified in approximately 40% of new installations citywide, up from about 20% three years ago.

## 2. Warm Neutrals Are Replacing Gray

Chicago's interior design aesthetic has shifted measurably from the cool gray tones that dominated 2018–2022 toward warmer neutrals — cream, linen, warm white, taupe, and warm beige. This shift is visible in window treatment fabric choices: cool white roller shades are being replaced by warm linen-tone fabrics; the blue-gray tones that were ubiquitous in Logan Square renovations are giving way to warm sand and terracotta-adjacent palettes. Wood blinds in light oak and white-washed finishes are benefiting from this shift as well.

## 3. Natural Materials in Chicago Vintage Buildings

Logan Square, Pilsen, Ukrainian Village, and other neighborhoods with active gut-rehab renovation activity are seeing strong demand for natural materials — woven grass shades, bamboo roller shades, real wood blinds — that complement the original character of vintage Chicago buildings rather than modernizing them. Woven wood shades (made from bamboo, jute, or rattan) have been particularly strong in this market segment, often paired with a sheer white liner for privacy.

## 4. Dual-Bracket Systems Are Now Standard in High-Rise Units

In Chicago's lakefront and city-view high-rises, dual-bracket systems — a solar shade for daytime use on the front bracket and a blackout roller shade on the rear — have become essentially the default specification. East-facing Lakeview and Edgewater units need solar during the day (to reduce lake glare while keeping the view) and blackout at night for sleep quality. We now quote dual-bracket systems as the base option for most high-rise primary living and bedroom windows.

## 5. The Roman Shade Resurgence

Roman shades, which fell out of fashion during the minimalist wave of the mid-2010s, are back strongly in Chicago's design-conscious markets. Logan Square homeowners are specifying bold-patterned Roman shades in kitchen windows. Lincoln Park and Lakeview homeowners are using Roman shades in fabric and pattern to add visual interest to renovated greystone interiors. The key to the current generation of Roman shades: simpler, flatter folds (the classic flat Roman or soft Roman rather than the fussy London or balloon styles), and more graphic patterns including stripes, geometrics, and large-scale botanicals.

## 6. Smart Home Integration as a Baseline Requirement

Younger Chicago homeowners increasingly specify smart home integration as a basic requirement rather than an optional upgrade. Alexa voice control, app-based scheduling, and sunrise/sunset automation are now table-stakes expectations for any motorized treatment. This has influenced hardware selection away from proprietary systems toward Somfy (the most universally compatible motor platform) and Lutron Caséta (native Apple HomeKit support). Installers who can program these integrations during installation — not leave programming as homework for the homeowner — are strongly preferred.

## 7. Chicago-Specific Pattern Choices

Chicago has a distinct interior design identity that differs from New York or Los Angeles. Navy blue remains extremely popular in Chicago living rooms — often seen in Roman shade fabric choices. Deep forest green has emerged as a strong alternative in 2025–2026. Geometric patterns with architectural references (grid patterns, linear repeats that echo Chicago's famous street grid) are popular in design-conscious renovations. Flat pattern rather than texture continues to dominate in fabric shade choices.
